HOW IT WORKS

Medical students , graduates and practicing physicians from all over the world will create accounts on MCAN, which will have a brief biography about them including their skill set that they can give advice on. These skills can include anything you think can help another medical student or graduate including but not limited to:

  • Improving your performance in medical school (basic science years)
  • Improving your performance in medical school (clinical years)
  • Applying for Research Electives abroad
  • Applying for Clinical Electives abroad
  • Advice about a specific specialty
  • Licensing exams (USMLE PLAB, and others)
  • Medical board exams (American board, Royal college exams, and others)
  • English exams (TOEFL and ILETS)
  • Applying for postgraduate clinical training (Residency and fellowship)
  • Applying for postgraduate Degrees (Master's Degree and PhD)
  • Experience about living abroad during electives , observerships or postgraduate training

Members will be able to search our vast online database for someone that has experience in an area that interests them. Members can then send career advice questions and even possibly arrange a career advising session with other more experienced members. The members will decide if they will do this career advising for free or will ask for a symbolic fee that will go as a donation for EAMTAR.
